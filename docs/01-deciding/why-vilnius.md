# Why Vilnius?

Vilnius is one of Europe's best-kept secrets — a medieval capital that offers
the beauty of Prague or Tallinn at a fraction of the price, without the crowds.

---

## The Case for Vilnius

### It's genuinely beautiful

The Old Town is one of the largest and best-preserved in all of Europe —
a UNESCO World Heritage Site packed with Gothic churches, Baroque courtyards,
and hidden passages. You can walk for hours and keep finding new corners.

### It's affordable

Compared to Western European capitals, Vilnius is remarkably cheap. A excellent
restaurant dinner costs €15–25. A coffee is €2–3. A hotel in the centre
starts from €50. Your money goes far here.

### It's compact and easy

The entire Old Town is walkable. The airport is 7 minutes by train from the
centre. English is spoken everywhere. There is no steep learning curve.

### It has a distinct personality

Vilnius is not trying to be Paris or Berlin. It has its own character —
shaped by Lithuanian, Polish, Jewish, Russian, and Soviet history all layered
on top of each other. The result is genuinely unique.

### Užupis alone is worth it

An entire neighbourhood that declared itself an independent republic in 1997,
complete with its own constitution, president, and army of 12 people.
Nowhere else in Europe has anything quite like it.

## Common Concerns

**"Isn't Lithuania far away?"**
Vilnius has direct flights from most major European cities. From London,
Berlin, or Warsaw it is a 2–3 hour flight. Ryanair, Wizz Air, and
airBaltic all serve the route cheaply.

**"Will I understand anything?"**
English is widely spoken, especially by anyone under 40. Menus, signs in
tourist areas, and transport apps are all available in English.

**"Is it safe?"**
Vilnius consistently ranks among the safest capitals in Europe. Violent
crime against tourists is extremely rare.

---

!!! tip "Still not sure?"
September is the single best month to visit — warm weather, autumn
colours beginning, no summer crowds, and lower prices. If you can only
go once, go in September.
