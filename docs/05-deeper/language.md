# Language & Culture

Lithuanian is one of the oldest living languages in the world —
linguists study it because it preserves features of ancient
Indo-European that other languages lost thousands of years ago.
You are unlikely to learn it in a weekend, but a few words
go a long way.

## Pronunciation Tips

- **č** sounds like "ch" in "church"
- **š** sounds like "sh" in "shoe"
- **ž** sounds like the "s" in "measure"
- **ū** sounds like a long "oo"
- **ė** sounds like "ay" in "say"

## Cultural Notes

**Lithuanians are reserved at first.** Do not mistake quietness for
unfriendliness — once you get past the initial reserve, people are
warm and generous. Smiling at strangers on the street is not the
local custom.

**Punctuality matters.** If you are meeting someone, be on time.
This applies to restaurant reservations too.

**Amber is the national gemstone.** You will see it everywhere.
Baltic amber is among the finest in the world and is genuinely
worth buying — just purchase from reputable shops rather than
street stalls.

**Nature is central to Lithuanian identity.** Mushroom picking,
berry foraging, and trips to the countryside are not tourist
activities — they are what Lithuanians actually do on weekends.
If someone invites you to their family's countryside house, accept.
