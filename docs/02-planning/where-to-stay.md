# Where to Stay

Vilnius is compact enough that location matters less than in larger cities.
But the neighbourhood you choose does shape your experience.

---

## Neighbourhood Overview

### Old Town (Senamiestis) — Best for first-timers

The heart of Vilnius. Staying here means everything is walkable —
churches, restaurants, cafes, and sights are all within minutes.

- **Best for:** First visit, couples, anyone who wants to walk everywhere
- **Price:** Highest — but still affordable by European standards
- **Vibe:** Historic, lively in the evenings, some noise on weekends

### Užupis — Best for atmosphere

The bohemian republic-within-a-city. Quiet, artistic, and charming.
A 10-minute walk from Cathedral Square.

- **Best for:** Repeat visitors, creative travellers, those who want
  a quieter base
- **Price:** Slightly lower than Old Town
- **Vibe:** Calm, artsy, riverside

### New Town (Naujamiestis) — Best for budget

Just west of the Old Town. More residential, with good transport links
and lower prices. Gedimino Avenue is the main commercial street.

- **Best for:** Budget travellers, longer stays, business visitors
- **Price:** Lower
- **Vibe:** Local, everyday, less touristy

### Šnipiškės (CBD) — Best for views

The modern glass district across the river. Great skyline views of the
Old Town. A 15-minute walk or one bus stop from the centre.

- **Best for:** Those who want modern hotels, business travellers
- **Price:** Mid to high
- **Vibe:** Contemporary, quiet at night

---

## Our Recommendation

**Stay in the Old Town or Užupis for your first visit.** The walkability
is genuinely valuable — you will wander back to your accommodation between
sights, rest, and head out again. This is much harder if you are a
20-minute commute away.
