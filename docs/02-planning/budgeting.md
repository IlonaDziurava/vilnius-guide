# Budgeting

Vilnius is one of the most affordable capitals in the European Union.
Here is what to realistically expect to spend.

---

## Daily Budget by Level

| Level     | Per day   | Includes                                                                                   |
| --------- | --------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Budget    | €40–60    | Hostel dorm (€15–20), supermarket meals or cheap eats (€15), free sights, public transport |
| Mid-range | €80–130   | 3-star hotel (€50–80), restaurant lunch and dinner (€25–40), one paid attraction           |
| Comfort   | €150–250+ | Boutique hotel (€100–150), fine dining (€50–80), guided tours                              |

## Money-Saving Tips

- **Eat lunch out, cook or supermarket for dinner** — lunch menus
  (set lunches) are common and excellent value at €7–10.
- **Use the M-Ticket app** for public transport — saves 35% vs cash fares.
- **Visit churches for free** — most of the most beautiful buildings
  in Vilnius have no entry charge.
- **Bolt over taxis** — always cheaper and more reliable than street taxis.
- **Stay just outside the Old Town** — 10 minutes walk saves 30–40%
  on accommodation.

---

!!! tip "ATMs"
Use bank ATMs — Swedbank, SEB, and Luminor have branches throughout
the city. Avoid standalone ATMs in tourist areas which charge high fees.
Always choose to be charged in Euros, not your home currency.
