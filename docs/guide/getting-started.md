# Getting Started

This page covers everything you need to know before your trip to Vilnius — visas, money, language, and practical essentials.

---

## Entry Requirements

=== "EU / EEA Citizens"
    No visa required. A valid national ID card or passport is sufficient. Lithuania is part of the Schengen Area.

=== "US / UK / CA / AU Citizens"
    No visa required for stays up to **90 days** within any 180-day period. A valid passport is required.

=== "Other Countries"
    Check the [Lithuanian Ministry of Foreign Affairs](https://www.urm.lt/en/) website for visa requirements specific to your nationality.

!!! warning "Passport Validity"
    Your passport should be valid for at least **3 months beyond** your planned departure date from the Schengen Area.

## Money & Budget

Lithuania uses the **Euro (€)**. Vilnius is affordable compared to most Western European capitals.

| Budget Level | Daily Spend (approx.) | What You Get |
|---|---|---|
| Budget | €40–60 | Hostel dorm, cheap eats, free sights |
| Mid-range | €80–130 | 3-star hotel, restaurant meals, paid attractions |
| Comfort | €150–250+ | Boutique hotel, fine dining, guided tours |

!!! tip "ATMs"
    ATMs are widely available throughout the city. Use bank ATMs (Swedbank, SEB, Luminor) to avoid high fees from independent ATM operators. Most restaurants and shops accept card payments.

## Language

The official language is **Lithuanian**, one of the oldest living Indo-European languages. However, English is widely spoken, especially by younger people and in tourist areas.

Useful Lithuanian phrases:

| Lithuanian | Pronunciation | English |
|---|---|---|
| Labas | *lah-bahs* | Hello |
| Ačiū | *ah-choo* | Thank you |
| Prašom | *prah-shom* | Please / You're welcome |
| Atsiprašau | *aht-see-prah-show* | Excuse me / Sorry |
| Kiek kainuoja? | *kyek kai-nuo-yah* | How much does it cost? |

## Connectivity

- **SIM cards** — available at the airport and convenience stores (Tele2, Bite, Telia). A prepaid SIM with 10GB data costs around €5–10.
- **Wi-Fi** — free Wi-Fi is available in most cafes, restaurants, hotels, and public spaces including the Old Town.

---

## Safety

Vilnius is a **very safe city** for tourists. Violent crime is rare. As in any city, take standard precautions:

!!! success "Safe Practices"
    - Keep an eye on your belongings in crowded areas
    - Use licensed taxis or Bolt/Uber apps
    - Avoid poorly lit streets in unfamiliar areas late at night

## Useful Apps

- **Bolt** — the dominant ride-hailing app (cheaper than taxis)
- **Trafi** — public transport routes and real-time schedules
- **Google Maps** — works excellently for navigation in Vilnius
- **Vilnius – the city in your pocket** — official city guide app
