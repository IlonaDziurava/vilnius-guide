    Language & Culture

    Lithuanian is one of the oldest living languages in the world — linguists study it because it preserves features of ancient Indo-European that other languages lost thousands of years ago. You are unlikely to learn it in a weekend, but a few words go a long way.

    Essential Phrases

    Lithuanian Pronunciation English
    Labas lah-bahs Hello
    Labas rytas lah-bahs ree-tahs Good morning
    Labas vakaras lah-bahs vah-kah-rahs Good evening
    Ačiū ah-choo Thank you
    Prašom prah-shom Please / You're welcome
    Atsiprašau aht-see-prah-show Excuse me / Sorry
    Taip tah-eep Yes
    Ne neh No
    Kiek kainuoja? kyek kai-nuo-yah How much does it cost?
    Kur yra...? koor ee-rah Where is...?
    Valgiaraštis val-gyah-rash-tis Menu
    Sąskaita sahs-kai-tah The bill
    Geras! geh-rahs Great! / Good!
    Sveikas! svay-kahs Cheers! (to a man)
    Sveika! svay-kah Cheers! (to a woman)

    Pronunciation Tips

    • č sounds like "ch" in "church"
    • š sounds like "sh" in "shoe"
    • ž sounds like the "s" in "measure"
    • ū sounds like a long "oo"
    • ė sounds like "ay" in "say"

    Cultural Notes

    Lithuanians are reserved at first. Do not mistake quietness for unfriendliness — once you get past the initial reserve, people are warm and generous. Smiling at strangers on the street is not the local custom.

    Punctuality matters. If you are meeting someone, be on time. This applies to restaurant reservations too.

    Amber is the national gemstone. You will see it everywhere. Baltic amber is among the finest in the world and is genuinely worth buying — just purchase from reputable shops rather than street stalls.

    Nature is central to Lithuanian identity. Mushroom picking, berry foraging, and trips to the countryside are not tourist activities — they are what Lithuanians actually do on weekends. If someone invites you to their family's countryside house, accept.
