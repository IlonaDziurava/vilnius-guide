    Old Town (Senamiestis)

    Vilnius Old Town is the historic heart of the city and one of the largest and best-preserved medieval old towns in Europe, designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1994.

    Area Facts

    • Size: 3.6 km² — the largest Old Town in the Baltic states
    • UNESCO status: World Heritage Site since 1994
    • Architecture: Gothic, Renaissance, Baroque, Classicism — all in one walk

    Must-See Sights

    Vilnius Cathedral & Bell Tower

    The Cathedral of St Stanislaus and St Vladislaus is the main Roman Catholic cathedral of Lithuania. The neoclassical building dominates Cathedral Square — the symbolic centre of the city.

    • Hours: Mon–Sat 07:00–19:00, Sun 07:00–20:00
    • Entry: Free
    • Tip: Look for the Stebuklas (Miracle) tile in the square — it marks the starting point of the Baltic Way, the 1989 human chain across all three Baltic states.

    Gediminas Tower

    The only remaining part of the Upper Castle, perched on a hill above the Old Town. Offers the best panoramic views of Vilnius.

    • Hours: Daily 10:00–19:00 (summer), 10:00–17:00 (winter)
    • Entry: €5 adults, €2.50 students
    • Access: Walk up (15 min) or take the funicular railway

    Gate of Dawn (Ausros Vartai)

    The only remaining gate of the old city wall. Inside is a small chapel housing a famous image of the Virgin Mary — one of the most important pilgrimage sites in Central and Eastern Europe.

    • Hours: Daily 06:00–19:00
    • Entry: Free
    • Note: Dress modestly when entering the chapel.

    St Anne's Church

    A stunning example of Flamboyant Gothic architecture, reportedly so admired by Napoleon that he wished he could carry it back to Paris in the palm of his hand.

    • Hours: Tue–Sat 10:00–18:00, Sun 10:00–15:00
    • Entry: Free

    Recommended Walking Route

    graph LR
    A[Cathedral Square] --> B[Pilies Street]
    B --> C[St Annes Church]
    C --> D[Bernardine Garden]
    D --> E[Uzupis Bridge]
    E --> F[Maironio Street back]
    F --> A

    Allow 2–3 hours for a leisurely walk with stops.

    Tips for Visiting

    Do's

    • Explore the courtyards hidden behind the main streets — some of the best surprises are off the beaten path
    • Visit early morning (before 09:00) for atmospheric, crowd-free streets
    • Walk up to Gediminas Tower at sunset for golden-hour views

    Don'ts

    • Don't rely on a car — most of the Old Town is pedestrianised
    • Don't skip the side streets — Stikliu, Sv. Mykolo, and Bernardinu are all beautiful
