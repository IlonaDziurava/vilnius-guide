    Užupis

    Užupis (meaning "beyond the river" in Lithuanian) is Vilnius's most eccentric and beloved neighbourhood. On April 1, 1997, it declared itself an independent republic — complete with its own president, army (of about 12 people), currency, and a constitution.

    The Republic of Užupis

    • Declared independent: April 1, 1997
    • Population: ~7,000
    • President: Romas Lileikis (artist and filmmaker)
    • National holiday: April 1 (April Fools' Day)
    • Constitution: Posted on the street in 23 languages

    The Constitution

    The Užupis Constitution is one of the neighbourhood's great delights. It is engraved on mirrored plaques on Paupio Street in dozens of languages. Some memorable articles:

    Article 1: Everyone has the right to live by the River Vilnelė, and the River Vilnelė has the right to flow by everyone.

    Article 12: Everyone has the right to be idle.

    Article 41: Everyone has the right to be happy.

    Article 42: Everyone is not obliged to be happy.

    Find the plaques

    Walk along Paupio Street beside the small Vilnelė river to read the full constitution. It's one of the most charming walks in Vilnius.

    What to Do in Užupis

    Angel of Užupis

    The neighbourhood's symbol — a bronze trumpet-playing angel on a column at the main intersection. Cast in 2002, it represents freedom and the creative spirit of the area.

    Galleries & Studios

    Užupis is packed with independent art galleries, artist studios, and craft workshops. Many are open to visitors. Look out for:

    • The Užupis Art Incubator
    • Small photography galleries on the back streets
    • Ceramic and textile workshops

    Cafes & Bars

    The neighbourhood has a relaxed, bohemian cafe culture. Grab a coffee, sit by the river, and watch the world go by.

    Crossing the Border

    On April 1st (the Republic's national holiday), the Užupis bridges become "border crossings" where guards stamp your passport with the Užupis stamp. Highly recommended if you happen to be in Vilnius on that date.

    "Border" Bridge Location
    Užupio tiltas Main entry from Old Town
    Malūnų tiltas Southern entry

    Getting There

    Užupis is a 10-minute walk from Cathedral Square. Walk east through the Old Town along Pilies Street, then turn left past St Anne's Church and cross the small Vilnelė river.

    Best Time to Visit

    Late afternoon on a weekday — the light on the river is beautiful, the streets are quiet, and you can browse the galleries without crowds.
